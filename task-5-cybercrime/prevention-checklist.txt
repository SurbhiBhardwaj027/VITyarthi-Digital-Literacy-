Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students in India

Use this practical checklist to protect your data, money, and identity online:

Account & Password Safety
1. Use strong, unique passwords for each account (at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
2. Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.
3. Avoid saving passwords on shared/public devices (like college labs or cyber cafés).

Device & App Security
4. Install apps only from official stores (Google Play Store / Apple App Store). Avoid APK downloads from unknown sites.
5. Keep your phone and laptop updated with the latest security patches.
6. Lock your devices with PIN, fingerprint, or face unlock—especially important on campus.

Safe Browsing & Social Media
7. Don’t click suspicious links in emails, WhatsApp messages, or Instagram DMs—even if they look like they’re from friends.
8. Limit personal information sharing (like phone number, address, college ID) on public profiles.
9. Verify job/internship offers—scammers often target students with fake opportunities.

Financial & UPI Safety (IMPORTANT)
10. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details—no legitimate service will ask for this.
11. Double-check UPI requests before approving—receiving money does NOT require entering your PIN.
12. Avoid scanning random QR codes sent by strangers—they may trigger payment requests instead of receiving money.

Awareness & Prevention
13.Be cautious on public Wi-Fi—avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on open networks.
14. Log out after using shared computers and clear browser history.
15. Watch for phishing signs: poor grammar, urgent messages, unknown senders.

Reporting Cybercrime
If you face fraud, harassment, or hacking:
• Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal
• Or call the Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud—faster action improves recovery chances)

Staying alert and following these steps can prevent most online scams.
